# 300 — Defining Your Direction

## What Makes a Good Direction?

A direction is the input that drives the entire northr.ai system. The quality of your adaptive plan is directly proportional to the quality of your direction.

### The AOST Formula

| Letter | Stands for | Question to ask |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Achievable | Can I realistically reach this within my constraints? |
| **O** | Outcome | Is this framed as a result, not an activity? |
| **S** | Specific | Is this concrete enough for AI to generate tasks? |
| **T** | Time-boxed | Does it have a clear deadline or review date? |

### Examples

| Weak Direction | Strong Direction |
|---|---|
| "Study Azure" | "Pass the AZ-500 exam by March 31" |
| "Work on the bid" | "Submit security bid by July 15" |
| "Get healthier" | "Run 3x per week for 8 consecutive weeks" |
| "Learn Crossplane" | "Complete learning-crossplane-inner-workings repo by end of month" |

---

## Managing Multiple Directions

northr.ai supports multiple simultaneous directions, but focus degrades as you add more.

**Recommended limit:** 2–3 active directions at any time

**Prioritise** directions explicitly so the AI knows how to allocate your time when there are conflicts.

**Example priority stack:**
1. (P1) Pass AZ-500 by March 31
2. (P2) Deliver Atlas IDP Crossplane MVP
3. (P3) Establish daily learning habit

---

## Direction Maintenance

Directions are not set-and-forget forever — review them:

- **Weekly:** Is this direction still valid?
- **Monthly:** Am I making meaningful progress?
- **Quarterly:** Does this direction still align with my larger goals?
