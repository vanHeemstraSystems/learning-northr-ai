# 100 — Getting Started with northr.ai

## Overview

northr.ai is an adaptive planning system built around a simple premise: you define your direction once, and AI handles the weekly scheduling details. This file walks you through your first steps.

---

## Step 1 — Create an Account

1. Navigate to [https://northr.ai](https://northr.ai)
2. Sign up with your email or an OAuth provider (Google / Microsoft)
3. Complete the onboarding questionnaire — this is how northr.ai learns your initial context

> **Tip:** Be honest in the onboarding. The quality of your initial adaptive plan depends on the context you provide.

---

## Step 2 — Understand the Interface

| Area | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Your live weekly plan — tasks, time blocks, and AI suggestions |
| **Direction** | Where you define and manage your goals and north star |
| **Weekly Plan** | AI-generated schedule for the current week |
| **Check-in** | Log your progress and signal blockers to the AI |
| **Settings** | Working hours, integrations, AI preferences |

---

## Step 3 — Define Your First Direction

A *direction* is northr.ai's term for your overarching goal or objective.

**Example directions:**

- *"Complete AZ-500 certification by end of Q2"*
- *"Ship the Atlas IDP Crossplane MVP"*
- *"Establish a consistent 30-minute daily deep work habit"*

**How to create one:**

1. Navigate to **Direction**
2. Click **+ New Direction**
3. Write a clear, outcome-focused goal statement
4. Set an optional target date
5. Save — the AI will now use this to inform your weekly plans

---

## Step 4 — Review Your First Weekly Plan

After saving your direction, northr.ai generates a weekly plan. Review it:

- Check that tasks map sensibly to your goal
- Adjust time blocks if your calendar has conflicts
- Accept or decline individual AI suggestions

---

## Key Takeaways

- northr.ai needs your direction to generate meaningful plans — be specific
- Your first week is calibration — the AI improves as you provide feedback via check-ins
- The interface is designed to minimise time spent in the tool itself
